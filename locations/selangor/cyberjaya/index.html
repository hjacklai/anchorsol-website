Building walls in Cyberjaya

Cyberjaya was built from 1995 onwards on the weathered granite plateau of the Selangor-Negeri Sembilan border, on what was previously palm oil estate land. The original master plan involved extensive cut-and-fill across the central precincts, leaving substantial engineered fill that modern developments must build over. Ground conditions vary by precinct: the older central core (Persiaran Multimedia, Cyberview corridor) has thicker engineered fill, while the newer perimeter (Cyberjaya 9, Persiaran APEC) sits closer to undisturbed weathered granite.

Cyberjaya is one of Malaysia's principal hyperscale data centre clusters and a designated MSC Malaysia cybercity. Hosts MMU (Multimedia University), Lim Kok Wing University, multiple Tier-1 colocation operators (TM, NTT, AIMS, Bridge, Iris), and continuing hyperscale roll-outs from Princeton Digital, GDS, Yondr. Active infrastructure: MRT2 Putrajaya Line stops at Cyberjaya City Centre + Cyberjaya Utara, ongoing road network upgrades along Persiaran APEC and Persiaran Multimedia.

Local authority: MPSepang (Majlis Perbandaran Sepang), with Cyberview Sdn. Bhd. as the city master developer. Nearby landmarks the local engineering community uses for site reference: Cyberjaya City Centre, Persiaran Multimedia, MMU campus, Lim Kok Wing University, Cyberjaya Mosque, Persiaran APEC, MRT2 Cyberjaya stations, KLIA / KLIA2 road corridor.

What we build in Cyberjaya

Hyperscale data centre platform walls dominate the recent project mix. Wall heights typically 5-12 m, with the bulk in the 6-9 m range. University campus boundary walls and engineered slope retention for hillside-fringe academic plots. Road embankment walls on Persiaran APEC arterial corridor. Drainage channel walls along Sungai Air Banun and tributaries threading through the city.

The wall systems available across these applications:

  • Anchored MSE wall, the AnchorSOL system: precast concrete facing panels, hot-dip galvanised tendons, discrete deadman block anchors, crusher-run backfill. Most economical above 5 m wall height. More on MSE wall fundamentals →
  • Reinforced Earth (RE) wall, the steel-strip variant: cruciform precast facing, ribbed steel strip reinforcement, granular sand backfill. More on RE walls →
  • Reinforced Soil (RS) wall, the geosynthetic variant: HDPE geogrid or PET woven grid reinforcement. More on RS walls →
  • RC retaining wall: for walls under 5 m or where space, programme, or aesthetic favours RC over MSE. More on RC retaining walls →

FAQ - retaining wall contractor in Cyberjaya

Do you build retaining walls in Cyberjaya?

Yes. AnchorSOL Wall Sdn. Bhd. delivers anchored MSE walls, RE walls, RS walls, and RC retaining walls in Cyberjaya and the wider Sepang district. Local authority is MPSepang (Majlis Perbandaran Sepang) with Cyberview Sdn. Bhd. as the city master developer. Delivery is from our Bukit Jelutong manufacturing facility, ~30 minutes road haulage to Cyberjaya.

Why is Cyberjaya seeing high retaining wall demand?

Cyberjaya is one of Malaysia's principal hyperscale data centre clusters. Multiple operators have campuses operating or under construction (TM, NTT, AIMS, Bridge Data Centres, Iris Data Centre, Princeton Digital, GDS, Yondr). Each campus needs substantial platform-edge retention - typically thousands of m² of wall face per facility - and the construction is on aggressive 18-24 month timelines that suit modular MSE delivery.

What ground conditions are in Cyberjaya?

Cyberjaya was built from 1995 onwards on former palm oil estate land on the weathered granite plateau. The site was extensively cut and filled during the 1995-2002 city construction phase, leaving substantial engineered fill across the central precincts. Modern data centre and commercial developments often build on this older engineered fill, requiring careful foundation design and sometimes ground improvement.

Which Cyberjaya agencies have you worked with?

We deliver to federal agencies (JKR, LLM via concessionaires) operating Cyberjaya road corridors, plus MPSepang for municipal projects, and direct to private data centre operators and university campuses. Project list available on request.
