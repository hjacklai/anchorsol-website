[ 00 ]MSE Wall, ExplainedMechanically Stabilized Earth

What is an MSE wall?

Mechanically Stabilized Earth is the modern engineering category that replaced concrete-gravity retaining walls. The retained soil itself is the structure. Steel reinforcement, deadman anchor blocks and a precast facing turn the fill mass into a single composite block that resists earth pressure from behind.

[ The principle ]

Earth + steel + facing = one composite mass.

Conventional gravity walls fight earth pressure with sheer concrete weight. MSE walls don't fight it, they recruit the soil itself. Horizontal steel reinforcement is laid at intervals through compacted backfill. Each layer stitches the soil mass together. A precast concrete facing holds the front face in line. The whole wall acts as a single block, lighter, taller, faster to build, and dramatically cheaper per square metre.

AnchorSOL® is an anchored variant of the MSE category. Where conventional MSE relies on friction along the full reinforcement length, AnchorSOL concentrates pullout resistance at a discrete deadman anchor block. Same family. Better mechanics. See the difference →
