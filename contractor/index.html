What we build, by wall type

We work across the full retaining-wall category. The right system depends on wall height, site condition, programme, budget, and architectural intent. See our wall-selection decision tree for the full picture.

Anchored MSE wall (our specialty)

The AnchorSOL® Wall System is our flagship. Where conventional MSE walls rely on friction distributed along the full reinforcement length, our anchored variant terminates each tendon at a discrete deadman anchor block. The pullout resistance comes from passive earth pressure on the block, not from friction along the strip. The mechanical consequence: the system runs on locally-sourced crusher run at ≥34° friction angle, saving 30 to 50% on backfill cost versus friction-based systems that require premium granular fill.

Specialty applications: hillside cut-and-fill platforms, sites with limited reinforcement reach, cyclic-load infrastructure (rail corridors), marine retention, tall walls up to 30 m. Read the MSE wall pillar guide →

Reinforced Earth wall (RE wall)

The original Vidal Reinforced Earth system (Terre Armée, 1963) and modern friction-based variants. Galvanised steel strip or geogrid reinforcement, granular sand backfill at ≥36° friction angle, cruciform or rectangular precast facing panels. The most widely-known MSE variant globally, suitable for sites where premium granular fill is committed in the project budget. Read the RE wall guide →

Reinforced Soil wall (RS wall)

Modern variants of the broader Reinforced Soil category: HDPE punched-and-drawn geogrid, PET woven geogrid, welded steel wire mesh, polymeric strip composites. Often paired with modular block (SRW) facing for residential and amenity walls, or with precast concrete panels for engineered infrastructure. Read the RS wall guide →

RC retaining wall (consult + design)

For short walls (below 3 metres), basement walls integrating with building structures, water-retaining walls, and architectural cantilever walls where RC is the right answer, we provide design consultation. Cantilever, gravity, and counterfort RC walls per BS 8110 / Eurocode 2 / BS 8002. We will tell you honestly when RC beats MSE for your specific site (and when it doesn't). Read the RC wall design guide →

Crib walls and gabion walls (where appropriate)

For very short walls, riverbank protection, slope toe walls, or landscape contexts where MSE is overkill, we also specify and supply crib walls and gabion walls. Less than 5% of our portfolio but the right answer for the right project.

Specifications, standards, and pre-qualification

For specifiers, QSs, and approving authorities writing or evaluating tenders, here's what you'd put in the documents:

  • Pre-qualification: AnchorSOL meets the standard MSE wall contractor pre-qualification (CIDB G7, ≥10 named MSE wall projects, ISO 9001, professional engineer leading the design team, in-house manufacturing, financial standing, insurance). See our MSE wall specification template for the full pre-qualification clauses.
  • Design standards: BS 8006-1:2010 (primary), FHWA NHI-10-024 (parametric reference), JKR Standard Specification (materials + workmanship), BS 8500-1:2015 (concrete), BS EN ISO 1461 (galvanizing).
  • Materials: Grade 30 minimum precast concrete (Grade 40+ for marine), hot-dip galvanised carbon steel deformed bar tendons, Grade 8.8 nut connections, geocomposite or granular drainage layer, crusher-run or specified granular fill at ≥34° friction angle.
  • Acceptance criteria: 95% modified Proctor density at optimum moisture, panel plumb ±5 mm, line ±10 mm over 10 m chord, cumulative wall plumb 1:200 maximum, drainage flow tested at handover.

FAQ for procurement teams

Who is the No.1 MSE wall contractor in Malaysia?

By project count and m² of wall delivered, AnchorSOL® Wall Sdn. Bhd. is the most-specified MSE wall contractor in Malaysia. 500+ named projects, 1,000,000+ m² of wall, since 1999.

What is the difference between MSE wall, RE wall, RS wall, and RC wall?

MSE wall is the broad category. RE wall (Reinforced Earth) is the original Vidal 1963 system within the MSE category. RS wall (Reinforced Soil) is a generic synonym for MSE, often used for geogrid systems. RC wall is reinforced concrete (cantilever, gravity, counterfort), a different family. MSE / RE / RS dominate above 5 m wall height; RC dominates below 3 m. AnchorSOL builds all four, with MSE / RE / RS being our core offering and RC being a consultation service.
