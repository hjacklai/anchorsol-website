Building walls in Kuching

Kuching straddles the Sarawak River, with the city centre on alluvial deposits and the inland hills (Gunung Serapi, Bako, Santubong) rising into folded sedimentary and intrusive granite. Peat soils dominate the south-western suburbs (Mata Kuching, Petra Jaya peat domes can be 4 to 12 m thick). Foundation design in Kuching depends on whether the site is alluvial flat, peat dome, or weathered intrusive — three very different design problems.

Kuching is the Sarawak state capital and the largest city in East Malaysia. Economy: oil and gas administrative hub (Petronas Sarawak), tourism (cat city, Sarawak Cultural Village, Bako and Kubah National Parks), and timber. Active infrastructure: Pan Borneo Highway Sarawak (S1-S11 packages, continuing rollout), Kuching Urban Transport System (LRT-like, planned), Sarawak Coastal Road extensions.

Local authority: DBKU (Dewan Bandaraya Kuching Utara) and MBKS (Majlis Bandaraya Kuching Selatan), bifurcated north-south of the Sarawak River. Nearby landmarks the local engineering community uses for site reference: Sarawak State Legislative Assembly (DUN), Astana, Fort Margherita, Kuching Waterfront, Sarawak Museum, Bako National Park, Kubah National Park, Santubong, Damai Beach, Petra Jaya, BDC, Tabuan Jaya, Kuching International Airport.

What we build in Kuching

Highway-embankment walls on the Pan Borneo Sarawak corridor and JKR federal roads. Foundation walls over peat ground requiring substantial ground improvement (PVDs + preload, stone columns, or in extreme cases mass-displacement piles). Hillside retention on the Santubong and Gunung Serapi foothills. Marine-edge walls on the Sarawak River and Kuching Waterfront.

The wall systems available across these applications:

  • Anchored MSE wall, the AnchorSOL system: precast concrete facing panels, hot-dip galvanised tendons, discrete deadman block anchors, crusher-run backfill. Most economical above 5 m wall height. More on MSE wall fundamentals →
  • Reinforced Earth (RE) wall, the steel-strip variant: cruciform precast facing, ribbed steel strip reinforcement, granular sand backfill. Henri Vidal's original 1963 system. More on RE walls →
  • Reinforced Soil (RS) wall, the geosynthetic variant: HDPE geogrid or PET woven grid reinforcement, segmental block or precast facing, crusher-run backfill. Faster to install on tight footprints. More on RS walls →
  • RC retaining wall, conventional reinforced-concrete cantilever or counterfort: for walls under 5 m, or where space, programme, or aesthetic favours RC over MSE. More on RC retaining walls →
