Building walls in Johor Bahru

JB Central sits on the coastal alluvial belt of the Tebrau Strait, with marine clays 5 to 20 m thick. The northern foothills (Tebrau, Pasir Pelangi, Permas Jaya, Plentong) climb into weathered granite. Sites near Danga Bay and the Causeway approach require marine-spec foundation due to saline groundwater.

JB is the Johor state capital and a primary entry point from Singapore via the Causeway and Second Link. Hosts the Iskandar Malaysia southern flank. Heavy infrastructure underway: RTS Link (Rapid Transit System) to Singapore, JB-Singapore Causeway upgrade, ongoing Bukit Chagar redevelopment, EDL extensions.

Local authority: MBJB (Majlis Bandaraya Johor Bahru). Nearby landmarks the local engineering community uses for site reference: JB Sentral, Bukit Chagar, Sultan Iskandar Building (CIQ Causeway), Danga Bay, Permas Jaya, Tebrau, Plentong, Larkin Sentral, Johor Bahru City Square, KSL City, R&F Princess Cove.

What we build in Johor Bahru

Causeway-approach and transit-hub walls (JB Sentral, RTS Link, Bukit Chagar). Marine-grade retention near the Tebrau Strait shoreline. Township platform walls on the Tebrau and Plentong foothills. Highway-embankment walls on the EDL and PLUS-South.

The wall systems available across these applications:

  • Anchored MSE wall, the AnchorSOL system: precast concrete facing panels, hot-dip galvanised tendons, discrete deadman block anchors, crusher-run backfill. Most economical above 5 m wall height. More on MSE wall fundamentals →
  • Reinforced Earth (RE) wall, the steel-strip variant: cruciform precast facing, ribbed steel strip reinforcement, granular sand backfill. Henri Vidal's original 1963 system. More on RE walls →
  • Reinforced Soil (RS) wall, the geosynthetic variant: HDPE geogrid or PET woven grid reinforcement, segmental block or precast facing, crusher-run backfill. Faster to install on tight footprints. More on RS walls →
  • RC retaining wall, conventional reinforced-concrete cantilever or counterfort: for walls under 5 m, or where space, programme, or aesthetic favours RC over MSE. More on RC retaining walls →

FAQ — retaining wall contractor in Johor Bahru

Do you build retaining walls in Johor Bahru?

Yes. AnchorSOL Wall Sdn. Bhd. delivers anchored MSE walls, RE walls, RS walls, and RC retaining walls in Johor Bahru and the wider Johor state. Local authority is MBJB (Majlis Bandaraya Johor Bahru). Our delivery is via the same precast manufacturing in Bukit Jelutong, Shah Alam, with project-managed haulage to Johor Bahru.

What wall type is best for Johor Bahru ground conditions?

Selection depends on the specific site. JB Central sits on the coastal alluvial belt of the Tebrau Strait, with marine clays 5 to 20 m thick. The northern foothills (Tebrau, Pasir Pelangi, Permas Jaya, Plentong) climb into weathered granite. Sites near Danga Bay and the Causeway approach require marine-spec foundation due to saline groundwater. Wall-system selection follows: hillside cuts favour anchored MSE; soft-foundation sites need ground improvement before MSE; urban-infill plots may need RC counterfort where space is constrained.

Which Johor Bahru agencies have you worked with?

We deliver to federal agencies (JKR, PLUS, LLM, KTMB) operating in Johor, plus the local authority MBJB (Majlis Bandaraya Johor Bahru) where municipal walls are specified. Project list available on request.

How does delivery to Johor Bahru work?

Precast facing panels are manufactured at our Bukit Jelutong (Shah Alam) facility, then hauled to Johor Bahru on flat-bed trucks. Tendon and anchor block production is co-located. A 3 to 4 person erection crew mobilises with the panels and completes 30 to 80 m² of wall per day on site.
