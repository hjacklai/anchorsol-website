Building walls in Subang Jaya

Subang Jaya sits on the alluvial Klang Valley plain with isolated hillside outcrops (notably Bukit Subang and the slope rising toward Damansara Heights). The central Subang Jaya / USJ / SS-series neighbourhoods sit on alluvial sandy clay 5-15 m thick over weathered shale, with ground improvement sometimes specified for taller walls. Hillside fringe sites in Bandar Sunway / Damansara direction transition to weathered granite saprolite with steeper grades.

Subang Jaya was elevated to city status (Bandaraya) in 2020 - one of the youngest cities in Malaysia. Dense Klang Valley commercial-and-residential with substantial education-cluster presence: Sunway University, Taylor's, INTI International College, Monash University Malaysia. Major commercial belt along Persiaran Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah and the LDP corridor. Adjacent to Sultan Abdul Aziz Shah Airport (Subang Skypark). LRT3 Shah Alam Line completed 2025 connects USJ to Bandar Sunway and onward to Shah Alam.

Local authority: MBSJ (Majlis Bandaraya Subang Jaya). Nearby landmarks the local engineering community uses for site reference: Sunway Pyramid, USJ 1-25, Bandar Sunway, Subang Skypark, 1 Utama (adjacent in Petaling Jaya), Persiaran Sultan Salahuddin Abdul Aziz Shah, LDP corridor, ELITE Expressway interchanges (USJ, Putra Heights), NPE corridor.

What we build in Subang Jaya

LDP / ELITE / NPE expressway corridor walls dominate the federal-road project mix. Township boundary and platform walls for major developments (Bandar Sunway, USJ subdivisions, Putra Heights). Education-campus and commercial perimeter walls along the major arterial belts. Drainage corridor and flood-mitigation walls along Sungai Damansara and Sungai Klang tributaries threading through the city.
